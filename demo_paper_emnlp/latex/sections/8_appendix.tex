\section{Router Configuration and Guards}
\label{app:router}

Table~\ref{tab:router-config} lists the default production configuration of
the hybrid active-glossary router as released in
\texttt{configs/autoterm\_slices.yaml}; environment variables can override
individual values per deployment. Guards are evaluated in order: warmup,
update interval, target-equals-active, index readiness, probe floors,
confidence, top-vs-runner-up margin, target-vs-current margin, post-switch
cooldown, and consecutive-window consistency. Uncertain windows keep the
current slice. The 640-window state-machine benchmark in
Section~\ref{sec:evaluation} ran under the stricter three-window
generated-target consistency setting; the released default was subsequently
retuned to two windows with a raised target-vs-current margin (0.30) based on
observed real-probe failure modes.

\section{Glossary-Scale Streaming Sweep}
\label{app:sweep}

Table~\ref{tab:sweep} reports the five-talk streaming sweep referenced in
Section~\ref{sec:evaluation}: five concatenated ACL 60-60 talks (3{,}442s of
audio) streamed through the live JSON WebSocket at latency multiplier 2, scored
against a curated 142-term technical gold set. Each inventory always contains
the full 238-entry curated ACL glossary; larger rows add Wikidata-mined AI/CS
candidates as distractors. These are manual-preset runs isolating inventory
scale (not \texttt{auto\_working} runs). Output term accuracy, BLEU, and
masked BLEU stay within a narrow band up to 100k terms, while retrieval
precision collapses --- the prompt channel fills with distractors long before
output quality visibly degrades, motivating compact routed active slices.

\section{Mixed-Domain Terminology under Union Inventories}
\label{app:mixed}

A glossary-channel comparison is only meaningful when the gold terms are
inside the retrieval inventory: the broad wiki slices cover 29/142 of the ACL
technical gold and 1/54 of the \texttt{medicine\_606} gold, so their term
accuracy mostly measures base-model retention. We therefore evaluate with
\emph{benchmark-aligned union inventories} at a fixed 10k denominator: the
byte-identical gold glossary (238 ACL / 212 hard-medicine terms) plus
seeded wiki fillers, mirroring the recipe of Appendix~\ref{app:sweep}
(gold coverage 142/142 and 54/54).

Table~\ref{tab:mixed} compares user-facing settings on a mixed
ACL\,$\rightarrow$\,medicine\,$\rightarrow$\,ACL playlist (600s per block,
live streaming at latency multiplier 2). Zero-setup \texttt{auto\_working}
exceeds \emph{both} single-domain fixed inventories on the combined gold:
it composes the right inventory per segment, switching twice with zero wrong
switches (37.4s / 25.0s after the boundaries; steady-state active-domain
accuracy 1.0; retrieval p95 101.6\,ms).

\section{Multilingual Terminology (En\,$\rightarrow$\,Ja/De)}
\label{app:multilingual}

The terminology resources are trilingual: the gold glossaries carry zh/ja/de
translations, a single MaxSim index serves every target language (translations
are resolved from the term list at prompt time), and the language pair selects
the per-language demo model. We repeat the mixed comparison on the full-length
ACL\,$\rightarrow$\,medicine\,$\rightarrow$\,ACL playlist for
En$\rightarrow$Ja (Table~\ref{tab:ja}). Zero-setup routing again has the best
combined term accuracy, and both switches are directionally correct with zero
wrong switches; switching is slower than for zh (115.3s/125.6s vs.\
37.4s/25.0s) because the topic-keyword channel is en/zh-tuned, which also
costs some BLEU relative to fixed inventories. For En$\rightarrow$De, routing
remains directionally correct (2/2 switches, zero wrong; 875.6s/39.2s) and
BLEU is on par across settings (35.9--37.6), but exact-match term accuracy is
unreliable under German inflection --- every setting, including
\texttt{none}, scores $\approx$0.11 --- so we omit the de term table.
Language-tuned topic keywords and inflection-aware term matching are
mechanical future work.
