\section{Limitations and Conclusion}
\label{sec:limitations}

The En$\rightarrow$Zh evaluation uses registered slices containing benchmark
terms, so it measures routing and retrieval competition rather than unseen
glossary induction. Routing can lag a topic boundary; a multi-slice budget only
mitigates this failure. Scale sensitivity also depends on language, retriever,
ambiguity, and prompt budget: 100k is an intermediate point and 1M a stress
condition, not a universal threshold. Broader multilingual validation remains
future work.

AutoTerm-SST provides zero-setup terminology retrieval over a modular offline
catalog. On mixed ACL and medicine talks, it matches known-domain quality,
beats no RAG, avoids 1M universal-glossary noise, and makes this behavior
visible in the demo.
