\section{Related Work}

\paragraph{Streaming speech translation.}
SST systems must translate partial speech while balancing quality and latency.
Prefix-to-prefix policies such as wait-\(k\) make this trade-off explicit
\citep{ma2019stacl}, attention-based policies refine read/write decisions
\citep{papi2023alignatt,papi2024streamatt}, SimulEval standardizes evaluation
\citep{ma2020simuleval}, and large-scale streaming models target direct
simultaneous translation \citep{seamless2023,zhang2024streamspeech}. Speech
language models make this setting more flexible
\citep{xu2025qwen3omni,ouyang2025infinisst}, but live deployment still requires
session management, incremental transport, and latency-aware prompting.
AutoTerm-SST focuses on adapting terminology during a stream; its contribution
is a system demonstration rather than a new model architecture.

\paragraph{Terminology-aware translation.}
Terminology control has long been studied in machine translation, usually
through user-provided dictionaries, constrained decoding, or training-time
exposure to terminology constraints \citep{hokamp2017lexically,dinu2019training};
terms and named entities are also a known weakness of speech translation
\citep{gaido2021moby}. Retrieval-augmented generation offers another way to
expose non-parametric memory at generation time
\citep{lewis2020retrieval,khandelwal2021nearest}. Many of these workflows assume
that the domain or terminology is known before translation begins. RASST couples
a speech-side MaxSim retriever with prompt-injected terminology
\citep{luo2026rasst}; AutoTerm-SST builds on this mechanism by adapting the
active terminology context from evidence observed during streaming.

\paragraph{LLM serving and demo systems.}
Modern LLM serving systems provide continuous batching and efficient generation
APIs \citep{kwon2023efficient,zheng2024sglang}, but a streaming speech demo also
needs microphone/file input, WebSocket transport, session cleanup, live evidence
rendering, and mock-mode testing. Translation Canvas
\citep{dandekar2024translation} targets offline translation analysis;
AutoTerm-SST provides a live serving layer while keeping model-specific code in
swappable agents.
